Group Reflection

Most members attended the group meetings and contributed ideas to the game making itself as well as risks, aims and goals. However, we do need some of our group members to be more proactive in listening to the recordings if they were unable to catch up to speed and hence be able to contribute. We also should have started on this assignment a little earlier than we did to avoid such stress. It was surprising that we were able to complete this assignment within 2 ½ weeks! Group work requires communication, patience, understanding and most of all, responsibility.

Member Reflection

Nayli

Assignment 3 was a bit confusing for me at first. Even though I found GB Studio and decided to present it as a software to use for our prototype, it was hard for me to understand the limitations of the software. I couldn’t design my own title screen and instead had to follow the pre-built palettes and elements available in the game. Eventually Kelvin and Jacky told me that we had to build a map using Tiled, a different software, but it was hard for me to understand how to use that application too. Overall, this assignment has made me appreciate how hard it actually is to design a game. The creative process and storyline of a game required a lot of teamwork.

Melanie

I was very lucky to have most of my group members be very responsible in completing their various assigned tasks each week. It definitely made my job a lot easier as I could log onto the google docs every day to edit things and make clarifications each time we had our group meetings. One thing I could have done more is perhaps to listen in more on the game prototype making as that is part of the assignment afterall. It was a challenge to combine all the game related points into paragraphs when I was not familiar with many of the terms, but that is exactly how I learned more about the game prototype making. It surprised me that we were able to come up with a working game prototype in such a short span of 2 ½ weeks! Teamwork and communication are so important for group assignments.
